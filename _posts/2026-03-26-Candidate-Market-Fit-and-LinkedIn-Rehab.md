## Candidate-Market Fit and LinkedIn Rehab

### Last Week
On last week's JSC call, we each shared our Candidate-Market Fit statements, and gave each other feedback. The next day, there was a LinkedIn Live for the NSA program, where recruiters reviewed several people's LinkedIn profiles and gave feedback. One big takeaway was that LinkedIn is a "lead gen" document, while the resume is a factual document of what you've done. (Not to say that LinkedIn isn't factual, but mainly that its purpose is for marketing you.) In LinkedIn, they recommend using relevant keywords, and being efficient with real estate on the page. 

This week, we began the "LinkedIn rehab" exercise, where we broke in two groups to review each person's profile, and give feedback based on the person's Mnookin 2-pager (what they're looking for) and Candidate-Market Fit (how they fit in the job market). The group as a whole had a takeaway about finding the balance between giving their profile character while telling a story, and being to-the-point and factual. I also realize I need to add in my technical skills from this program (as Professor Guinn mentioned in my resume review), and update language to be more directed toward Technical Program Manager roles, which is what I'm targeting. My About section could use a refresh to show more character in the language, what I'm looking for, and not just be a block paragraph. I also may want to add more "skills" (the feature) per each role, although I want to research this more to see if it's useful for recruiters or not. Last, each of my roles have really long descriptions (taken directly from my resume). I like this as a way to remember what I actually did at each role, but I realized I could save that info to a Google doc so I don't lose it, and then update LinkedIn to be more in that marketing format. And like I've mentioned previously with my resume, I should try to "lead with results" in the bullets for each role.
