My main takeaways from the readings this week were:

In discussion of what makes the self (body or mind), they compared it to hardware vs software. They discussed what the boundaries of self are - can a body have a different self at different times in their life (a boy vs soldier, etc.). An analogy is like if hardware is wiped of software and a new system is installed, is it the same self? What about installing a software on multiple machines? Are they the same?
 The book also discussed how robots perceive space, and mentioned how they may need multiple frames to be able to coordinate how far away an object is, or what angle something is. I believe VR systems perceive space in a similar way.
Much of the logic concepts we learned in CS were covered, including prepositional logic with p and q truth functions, which corresponds to Boolean logic in computers. Contradictions, proofs and combinatorics were also covered.
