## Philosophy and CS

### Last Week
I took a hard turn on my project this week, since I want to take a break from job activities while I finish the semester. Recently I'd seen a few articles mentioning the "new-found" importance of Philosophy majors in this age of AI. Years ago in my first degree program, I'd noticed the pattern of CS people with a Philosophy degree or dual degree, and wondered why. For many years I had a bit of an aversion to philosophy, seeing it as a waste of time. However, in our program (and particularly in Discrete Structures and AI), I kept noticing the mathematicians that invented/discovered some important ideas were also philosophers.

I decided to use my remaining three weeks to dive into the world of philosophy and see how it relates to CS. I found the audiobook Think by Simon Blackburn (an overview of Western philosophy) and will listen to a little over 3 hours per week. This week I listened to the chapters Knowledge, Mind, Free Will, and part of The Self, and took notes on my phone's notes app. I was planning to do a write up but there were so many ideas covered that it would be quite lengthy. 

My main takeaways were:
- Philosophy is about asking questions about why we ask the questions we do. It's a level up from accepting that things are the way we think they are. The "crazy" questions asked by philosophers in the past ("is the world real or a dream?" "are mind and body separate?" "do we have free will?") are how we got to the knowledge that we have today. By continuing to ask seemingly outlandish questions is how we make new discoveries or understandings. This was explained well in this interview I watched as well with the creator of the podcast "Philosophize This!", which is highly recommended as accessible and engaging for beginners. They mention how the Ph in PhD is for philosophy for a reason - since PhDs are doing thinking at the highest levels in their field.
- There are sensory/perceptive (Locke) vs scientific (Descartes) methods of understanding. Descartes' work is very applicable to causational programming logic.
- Many philosophers have thought over- what is mind and body and do they effect each other? This is very relevant to AI. It also made me think how strange it is that both the brain and computers work by electrical impulses. I also wonder if robots are needed to fully represent humans.
- The discussions on determinism and whether we are free to effect the future or whether it is predetermined was interesting. Now I understand in Data Science why probabilities without chance are called deterministic, which follows this line of thinking.
