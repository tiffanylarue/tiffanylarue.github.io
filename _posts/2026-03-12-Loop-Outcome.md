I'd drafted this write up for my JSC about my interview prep and thought I'd share it here...

I ended up doing another mock interview before the interview. The marathon of the process was intense, but overall I was happy with how the interviews went. I watched both of the mock interview recording videos the day before, which was helpful for review and mindset. I ended up recording audio on my phone of me talking through all my STAR stories and then I played it back a couple times while I was out walking, until the stories stuck better. I drafted quick answers for many of the common questions, but didn't find time to convert them into full STAR format. It was ok, because I had some of the metrics/outcomes in my mind (at some point I want to get these in more detail on paper).

The interviews were interesting. I had three the first day, and two the second. I felt that all went pretty well, except one question with a cross-functional partner (the second interview). Everyone had their own unique interview style, and the question that didn't go well - I feel was partly due to the interviewer. He wasn't prepared for the call, and when I wanted to circle back to an earlier question after we'd covered the main things, he preferred to end the call early after only 40 mins instead of the hour. That dented my confidence, but luckily all the other interviewers made sure they got all parts of the STAR answered, "helped" with the format, and stayed for the full hour. I got quite a few of the questions that I'd prepared for in the mocks.

I felt it was good to have it over two days because I was exhausted after Day 1. The first two were back to back, then 30 min break, then one more. For the second day, I made a list of the strongest stories I hadn't used yet, and stuck to those for the remaining interviews. I also popped my stories in ChatGPT and it told me areas I could strengthen my stories. I didn't have time for that, but will probably do that in the future!

Other preparation...
I cleared my schedule the day before the interviews, so I didn't have school on my mind, and could just think about STAR stories.

Another thing I learned in this process is the importance of your interview outfit. Of my interview tops, I had only one that I really liked, felt confident in, and wasn't distracted by (i.e. is the pattern weird/still in style? Is it professional enough? etc.). I went to the mall several times looking for another outfit to wear the second day, didn't find anything, but ended up making friends with two ladies, working at Ann Taylor, and Nordstrom. The lady at Nordstrom was saying the most important thing is that you feel comfortable and confident in the outfit, and can be you! I ended up finding I could use a top I already had with a safety pin adjustment.

I used my standing desk to stand during each interview, which was helpful. Someone had reminded me about the Superman pose, which made me think of standing. I think it makes speaking / airflow come more easily.
